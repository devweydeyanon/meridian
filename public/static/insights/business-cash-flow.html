5 ways to manage cash flow as a growing business

GrowthMarch 8, 20266 min read

Cash flow is the lifeblood of every business. Even profitable companies fail when they can't cover day-to-day expenses. Here are five practical strategies to keep your cash flow healthy as you scale.

1. Invoice faster, collect sooner

The gap between delivering work and getting paid is where cash flow problems start. Send invoices the same day you complete work. Offer small discounts for early payment (2% net 10 is standard). Use automated invoicing tools that send reminders before payments are due.

2. Build a cash reserve

Aim to keep 3–6 months of operating expenses in a business savings account. This buffer protects you from seasonal dips, late-paying clients, and unexpected costs. Even setting aside a fixed percentage of each month's revenue builds this cushion over time.

A Meridian Business Savings account earns 3.50% APY on your reserves while keeping funds accessible when you need them.

3. Manage expenses proactively

Review recurring costs quarterly. Cancel unused subscriptions. Negotiate better terms with vendors — many will offer discounts for annual prepayment or loyalty. Separate needs from wants: every dollar saved on overhead is a dollar available for growth.

4. Use a business line of credit strategically

A line of credit isn't emergency funding — it's a planning tool. Use it to bridge predictable gaps like seasonal slowdowns or the timing mismatch between paying suppliers and collecting from customers. Only draw what you need, and repay quickly to minimize interest.

5. Forecast, don't guess

Build a simple 13-week cash flow forecast. List expected income and expenses week by week. Update it weekly. This gives you early warning of potential shortfalls and time to act — whether that means accelerating collections, delaying a purchase, or drawing on your credit line.
